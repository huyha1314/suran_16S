1 Project Workflow

This report details the taxonomic composition, diversity metrics, and predicted functional pathways of the microbial communities identified via 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing. Click on any section below to expand and view the details of the pipeline.

Note1. Quality Control & Preprocessing
  • Process: Raw sequencing reads were quality-filtered and adapters were removed using Cutadapt. Quality was assessed using FastQC and aggregated via MultiQC.
Note2. ASV Inference & Taxonomy
  • Process: The DADA2 pipeline was used to filter, dereplicate, and denoise the reads to resolve exact Amplicon Sequence Variants (ASVs). Taxonomy was assigned against a reference database.
Note3. Diversity Analysis
  • Process: Alpha diversity (within-sample) was calculated to assess community richness and evenness. Beta diversity (between-sample) was analyzed using Principal Coordinates Analysis (PCoA) to identify structural shifts across groups.
Note4. Biomarker & Functional Prediction
  • Process: Differential abundance testing highlighted specific taxa driving the variations between cohorts. PICRUSt2 was utilized to predict the metabolic and functional potential of the microbial communities based on their 16S profile.

7 Beta Diversity (Between-Sample)

Tip

Beta diversity measures the structural differences between microbial communities across multiple distance metrics (e.g., Bray-Curtis for abundance, UniFrac for evolutionary distance). Samples that cluster closely together share similar microbiomes. Significance is calculated via PERMANOVA, and variance assumptions are validated using PERMDISP.
